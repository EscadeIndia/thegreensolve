TGS LCA Tool – Carbon Footprint Calculation Methodology

1. Overview

The TGS LCA Tool is designed to calculate the cradle-to-gate carbon footprint of products, providing manufacturers and brands with an accurate estimation of greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions associated with their products. The tool follows internationally recognized standards for carbon footprint calculation and life cycle assessment (LCA), ensuring methodological robustness and consistency.

The carbon footprint calculations are based on average emission factors for materials, energy consumption, and transportation. Key parameters considered include:

  • Product category and total weight
  • Material composition (including the weight of unique materials used in each product and its packaging)
  • Geographical locations of suppliers, manufacturing facilities, and distribution centers
  • Transport distances and modes (e.g., road, rail, air, or sea transport)
  • Energy sources used in production

The TGS LCA Tool is intended for product manufacturers and brands to estimate the environmental impact of their products. While it provides an industry-aligned and reliable estimation, it is not a substitute for a full ISO-compliant Life Cycle Assessment (LCA).

2. Standards and Methodological Framework

The TGS LCA Tool aligns with the following internationally recognized standards for carbon footprint and LCA methodologies:

  • ISO 14040 & ISO 14044 – Life Cycle Assessment: Principles and Framework
  • ISO 14067 – Carbon Footprint of Products: Requirements and Guidelines
  • GHG Protocol Product Life Cycle Accounting and Reporting Standard
  • PAS 2050 – Specification for the Assessment of the Life Cycle Greenhouse Gas Emissions of Goods and Services

These standards ensure that the methodology adheres to best practices in data collection, emission factor application, and system boundary definition.

3. System Boundary: Cradle-to-Gate Approach

The TGS LCA Tool calculates emissions from the cradle-to-gate phases of a product’s life cycle, covering:

  • Raw material extraction and processing
  • Manufacturing and assembly
  • Packaging production
  • Transportation of materials and products up to the factory gate

This approach accounts for emissions under the operational control of the manufacturer but does not include distribution, retail, product use, or end-of-life disposal. For a full cradle-to-grave assessment, an extended LCA study is required.

4. Emission Factor Sources

The emission factors used in the TGS LCA Tool are sourced from validated and internationally recognized third-party datasets, including:

  • Ecoinvent – A leading LCA database providing high-quality, science-based emission factors
  • DEFRA (UK Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs) – Comprehensive emission factors for energy, transport, and waste management
  • IPCC (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change) Guidelines – Global Warming Potential (GWP) values used for converting emissions to CO₂-equivalent (CO₂e)
  • GHG Protocol Databases – Industry-relevant datasets for corporate and product-level emissions accounting
  • National and Regional Databases – Where applicable, local datasets are used to improve accuracy in region-specific calculations

The tool applies Global Warming Potential (GWP) factors from the latest IPCC Assessment Reports to ensure consistency in emissions reporting.

5. Calculation Approach and Data Integrity

The final carbon footprint is expressed in kg CO₂e and calculated using a structured Life Cycle Inventory (LCI) approach, where input data is systematically processed to generate reliable emission results. The methodology ensures:

  • Transparency in data sources and assumptions
  • Consistency with international LCA methodologies
  • Accuracy through the use of regionally relevant emission factors
  • Data validation from authoritative third-party sources
6. Limitations and Intended Use

The TGS LCA Tool is designed to provide an acceptably accurate carbon footprint estimation suitable for self-declaration by product manufacturers or brands. However, it is not intended for regulatory compliance, environmental product declarations (EPDs), or comparative assertions unless verified by an independent LCA expert.

While every effort has been made to ensure data accuracy and methodological consistency, TGS Solutions and its partners do not guarantee the absolute accuracy of third-party emission factor sources. Users requiring an in-depth, ISO-certified LCA study should consult a professional LCA practitioner or certification body.

7. Conclusion

The TGS LCA Tool offers a scientifically grounded, industry-aligned approach to carbon footprint calculation, helping manufacturers understand and manage their environmental impact. By following established LCA standards and using high-quality emission factors, the tool provides a reliable cradle-to-gate footprint assessment, enabling companies to make informed decisions for sustainability and emissions reduction.
